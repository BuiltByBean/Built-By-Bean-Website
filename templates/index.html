Here's what I'm here to fix

The problem with "one size fits all"

The market is flooded with generic software that claims to do everything. Most of it is built for the average business, which means it's not really built for yours. You end up paying for features you don't need and working around the ones you do. I build the opposite of that.

The industry wants renters, not owners

You pay to get started, then you pay every month to keep using something that should already be yours. The whole model is designed so you never actually own anything. I don't think that's right.

One developer. No layers.

I'm a solo developer, not an agency. There's no sales team, no account managers, no middlemen. When you work with me, you're talking directly to the person writing the code, and I genuinely care whether it works for you.

Cost shouldn't be the barrier

You're trying to grow your business and improve the bottom line. The last thing you want is another expense. But anywhere you're doing manual work, juggling between systems, or burning hours on something repetitive, that's costing you more than software ever would. And when I say I'll work with your budget, that's not a tagline. I mean it.

You've got three options

Every business runs on one of these three setups. Here's the honest tradeoff on each.

Spreadsheets & manual work
  • Free to start
  • You already know how it works
  • Eats hours of your week
  • Breaks every time a cell gets dragged
  • The whole system lives in one person's head
  • Caps out hard at one or two people
Off-the-shelf software
  • Quick to sign up
  • Packed with features
  • You bend your workflow to match theirs
  • Pay forever, never own a thing
  • Paying for features you'll never touch
  • Your data is locked in, and leaving isn't easy
Custom built
  • Fits how you already work
  • You own it. Pay once, keep it forever
  • Automates the manual stuff
  • No ongoing cost for the product, no per-user pricing
  • Grows with your business
  • Your data is yours. Take it anywhere, anytime
